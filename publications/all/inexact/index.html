Routley Star and Hyperintensionality

Sergei Odintsov and Heinrich Wansing

Compares HYPE to Heyting-Ockam logic. Contains a characterization of HYPE as identical to a modal system introduced by Moisil. From the perspective of truthmaker semantics, these results can be understood as results about (a certain kind of) inexact truthmaker semantics.

Keywords:

  • logic
  • modality
  • theory

In: Journal of Philosophical Logic 50: 33-56

10.1007/s10992-020-09558-5

Truthmaker Semantics for Relevant Logic

Mark Jago

Presents an exact truthmaker semantics for the relevance logic <b>R</b>. Note especially the alternative treatment of disjunctions.

  • logic
  • relevance
  • exact truthmaking

10.1007/s10992-019-09533-9

HYPE: A System of Hyperintensional Logic (With an Application to Semantic Paradoxes)

Hannes Leitgeb

New system hyperintensional semantics/logic, which from the perspective of truthmaker semantics can best be viewed as a form of inexact truthmaker semantics. Contains a very interesting philosphical application.

A Plea for Inexact Truthmaking

Michael Deigan

Provides an interesting argument for the primacy of inexact truthmaking: the claim that we can define exact in terms of inexact truthmaking and not the other way around (which is what we standardly assume in the exact truthmaking literature). Best enjoyed together with (Leitgeb 2020).

  • theory

Facts and Tautological Entailments

Bas C van Fraassen

This is the first article in which the exact truthmaker clauses appear (p. 484). However, van Fraassen uses them to provide an inexact truthmaker semantics for what’s known today as the logic of First Degree Entailment (or, in his time, tautological entailment).
